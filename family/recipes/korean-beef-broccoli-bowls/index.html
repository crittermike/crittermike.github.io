Korean Beef + Broccoli Bowls

Protein
60g
Calories
600
Kids
Yes

Ingredients

Tap to check off as you go
  • Beef
  • 2 lb ground beef (93% lean works great)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil (for cooking)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated (or 1 tsp ground)
  • Sauce (whisk in a jar/bowl)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar (or apple cider in a pinch)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (1/2 tsp for kid-friendly)
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • Broccoli
  • 2 large heads broccoli, cut into bite-size florets (~6 cups)
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or vegetable)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tbsp water
  • Serve with
  • 3 cups jasmine rice, cooked (~1.5 cups dry)
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Sriracha on the table
  • Optional: kimchi or quick-pickled carrots for crunch

Method

  1. Start rice first (longest part).
  2. **Broccoli, separate pan.** Big skillet, medium-high, neutral oil hot. Broccoli in single layer, don't crowd. Sit 2 min for char. Toss, another 2 min. Splash 2 tbsp water, lid on 90 sec. Lid off, salt. Should be bright green with brown edges, still has snap. Pull off heat.
  3. **Beef, separate skillet.** Sesame oil, medium-high. Brown beef hard, break it small, 6-8 min. Drop heat to medium. Push beef aside, garlic + ginger in empty space, 30 sec until fragrant. Stir together.
  4. Pour sauce in, stir. Thickens in ~90 sec from cornstarch. If too thick, splash of water.
  5. Taste. Flat? More soy. Too salty? Pinch more brown sugar.
  6. Dump broccoli into beef pan at the end, toss 30 sec to coat in sauce.

Notes & pitfalls

Morning Prep (optional, saves 15 min in evening)

  1. Whisk full sauce in a jar, refrigerate. Shake before using.
  2. Mince garlic + ginger together, small container, fridge.
  3. Cut broccoli into florets, gallon ziploc, fridge crisper.
  4. Measure dry rice + water into rice cooker pot, leave on counter. Don't pre-cook the beef or broccoli — reheating ruins both.

Assembly

Rice in bowl. Beef + broccoli on top. Sesame seeds over the top. Sriracha for anyone over 12.

Notes

  • Two pans for broccoli + beef is non-negotiable. Cooking broccoli in beef pan = soggy gray broccoli from sauce moisture.
  • 93% lean is fine here — sauce carries the richness.
  • Leftovers: cold beef on rice next day is unreasonably good. Or fry an egg on top.
  • Sub English cucumber sliced for cold crunch contrast if desired.

Kid Reactions (expected)

  • Thomas: deconstructed, beef and rice not touching, no green stuff visible.
  • William: will pick around broccoli; deal = 3 pieces minimum.
  • Henry + Charlie: inhale and ask for seconds.
  • Nancy: yes. No tzatziki.
