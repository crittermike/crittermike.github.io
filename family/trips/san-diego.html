About the venue

The Junipero Serra Museum sits on a hilltop in Presidio Park, overlooking Mission Valley and the San Diego River. Built in 1929 in Spanish Colonial Revival style, it marks the site where Father Junipero Serra established the first Spanish mission in California in 1769.

Panoramic views, white stucco architecture, gardens. The hilltop setting means golden-hour photos will be excellent.

While we're there

★ = Lauren's pick
  • Balboa Park★ Lauren
    San Diego, CA · (619) 239-0512
    San Diego's Central Park, bigger. 1,200 acres, 18 museums, gardens, hiking, the San Diego Zoo. Pack a lunch and sit where the planes fly ~50 ft overhead descending into Lindbergh Field. The Prado (1914/15, expanded 1934/35) has the Spanish Colonial architecture from the Panama-California Exposition.
  • Cabrillo National Monument & Lighthouse★ Lauren
    1800 Cabrillo Memorial Dr, San Diego, CA 92106
    National monument on the tip of Point Loma ($15-20 entry). 270° views of downtown, Coronado, the north beaches, and the Pacific. Tide pools at the base are open to visitors.
  • Windansea Beach★ Lauren's favorite SD beach
    6600 Neptune Pl, La Jolla, CA 92037 · (619) 221-8899
    Blue water, yellow-white sand, sandstone caves to explore if the tide allows. Picturesque surf spot with the historic palm-covered surf shack.
  • Embarcadero
    1492 N Harbor Dr, San Diego, CA 92101
    Cruise hub with Seaport Village (seafood, souvenirs), The Headquarters (eateries and boutiques in an old police command post), the USS Midway Museum (aircraft carrier with flight sims and restored planes), and Waterfront Park.
  • Marston House
    3525 7th Ave, San Diego, CA 92103 · (619) 297-9327
    Historic Arts & Crafts mansion from 1905.
  • Heritage Park
    2454 Heritage Park Row, San Diego, CA 92110
    Restored Victorian-era homes from the 1880s-1890s, in Old Town.
