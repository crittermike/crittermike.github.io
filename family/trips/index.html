Lake Hartwell

A 5,000 sq ft lakefront house on Lake Hartwell, near the Georgia/South Carolina border. Five nights, a private covered dock, and room for a big group.

The trip

A lakefront house on Lake Hartwell, about 1.5 hours from Greenville via I-85. The private dock is covered, has deep water for swimming, and fish bite right off it. A pontoon rental for the middle of the week rounds it out.

The house

An Airbnb Guest Favorite. Reviewers call out the well-stocked kitchen and comfortable beds. A paved walkway runs from the house down to the covered dock, so all ages can get to the water easily.

The boat

Booked

A pontoon is booked for the middle three days through Boatsetter. Self-drive, no captain. Pickup is on the Anderson, SC side of the lake, about 50 minutes from the house.

The boat
2014 Premier 220 SunSation RE
Tritoon (triple tube), 150hp, 22 ft. Seats up to 12. Bluetooth stereo, swim ladder, anchor, and life jackets aboard. Pulls tubes easily; wakeboarding is doable but not aggressive.
Schedule
Tue Jul 7 – Thu Jul 9
Pickup Tue Jul 7 at 8:30 AM. Return Thu Jul 9 by 6:00 PM. Three full days on the water.
Good to know
Fuel isn't included, so top it off before returning to avoid a fee. Don't be late bringing it back. Bring the add-ons you want (tubes, paddleboards, cooler) or arrange them ahead of pickup.

Food & supplies

In Hartwell, ~20 min

The house is out on the lake, so town is about a 20-minute drive. Worth one big grocery run on the way in. A few places we'd point you to:

Groceries & supplies

  • Ingles MarketFull grocery store. Open early to late. Best for a big stock-up run.
  • Walmart SupercenterGroceries plus anything else you forgot to pack.

Eating out

  • Boat House GrillWaterfront bar and grill at the marina. Come by boat or car, live music on weekends. Seasonal.
  • Downtown CafeLongtime local spot downtown, Greek and Italian. Reservations a good idea.
  • Backstreet BBQCasual barbecue in town. Easy dinner for a crowd.

What to bring

  • SwimsuitsAnd beach towels. The house provides bath towels only.
  • SunscreenJuly in the South is hot. Hats too.
  • Bug sprayLakefront in July.
  • Fishing gearIf you have it. Otherwise rent on the boat.
  • SnacksNearest stores are ~20 min away.
  • CoolerFor the dock and the pontoon.
  • FlashlightsFor the walk down to the dock at night.
  • GamesThe house has some; bring favorites for downtime.
